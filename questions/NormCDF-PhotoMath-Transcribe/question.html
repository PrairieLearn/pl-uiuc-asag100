You are designing a machine that produces parts whose lengths follow a normal distribution with mean $\mu = {{params.mu}}$ and standard deviation $\sigma = {{params.sigma}}$. However, only parts with lengths between {{params.minlen}} cm and {{params.maxlen}} cm can be used. Your task is to calculate the "yield rate", the expected percentage of parts that fall within this acceptable range. You can do this by integrating the normal distribution's probability density function (PDF), $$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma^2}} e^{-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2}}$$ between {{params.minlen}} and {{params.maxlen}}.   3. If you run $N$ Monte Carlo iterations and $N_p$ of the sampled points fall within the region of interest, what is the estimated yield rate?
